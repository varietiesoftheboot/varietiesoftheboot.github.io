Varieties of the Boot

A local, multidisciplinary community for the study, documentation, and preservation of languages and dialects of Italy through responsible development of language and speech technologies.

Are you a speaker of a language variety of Italy and interested in contributing? Please reach us!

The Linguistic Heritage of Italy is Endangered

Italy is characterized by a one-of-a-kind linguistic diversity landscape in Europe, which encodes local knowledge, cultural traditions, artistic expression, and history of its speakers. However, according to the UNESCO's Atlas of Worldâ€™s Languages in Danger, over 30 language varieties in Italy (and their many variants) are at risk of disappearing within few generations.

Speaker-centric language technology support

Language technology has a main role in supporting endangered varieties. However, available resources for Italy's varieties are scarce and unrepresentative. Nevertheless, research in language technology has to date considered them as a data commodity, diregarding the nature of Italy's varieties and their speakers' needs. Are Italy's varieties just another input to a language model?

We advocate for a shift in the paradigm from machine-centric to speaker-centric language technology to responsibly support the preservation, documentation, and study of Italy's varieties. We believe this will be only possible through local knowledge sharing and multidisciplinary collaboration of native speakers of language varieties of Italy. To learn more, read this paper.

Join the Community

We welcome native speakers passionate about Italy's varieties which share the mission of Varieties of the Boot, regardless of their background or expertise. Researchers (linguists, computer scientists, anthropologists, etc.), members of cultural institutions, interested individuals, and regular speakers are equally welcome.

Why should I join?

Through Varieties of the Boot, you can connect with other community members and benefit from multidisciplinary collaboration to support your own variety and be exposed to others' local knowledge. Your contributions will be valuable to support other varieties, to identify best practices and common frameworks. Community-wide efforts will be submitted to relevant workshops/conferences acknowledging active community members with authorship. We are going to create a slack messaging community soon.

How can I contribute?

There are many ways you can contribute! Some examples include:

  • Collection of publicly-available resources for Italy's varieties to build a catalogue
  • Assessment of quality/representativeness of existing resources for your own variety
  • Enrichment of existing resources with metadata (e.g., subvarieties, domains)
  • Annotation of existing resources with linguistic information (e.g., morphology, UD)
  • Collection of new spoken or written data (e.g., field linguistics, online sources)
  • Development of an interoperable data loader to easily work with existing resources
  • Documentation of varieties according to linguistic phenomena and diatopic variation
  • Raise awareness on the linguistic heritage of Italy on social media
  • Other ideas? We would be happy to know!
